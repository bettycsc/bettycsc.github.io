大暑与铜的焰色反应
Great Heat and Flame Color Reaction of Copper

大暑，正值一年中最为炽热的时节，骄阳似火，大地仿佛被置于巨大的蒸笼之中。此时，来做铜的焰色反应实验，就如同将这酷热推向了极致。
Great Heat is the hottest season of the year. The sun is blazing, and the earth seems to be placed in a huge steamer. At this time, conducting the flame color reaction experiment of copper is like pushing this extreme heat to the limit.

当铜在火焰中接受高温的洗礼，瞬间绽放出耀眼的绿光，那绿光恰似大暑时节蓬勃到极致的生命力，在高温的激发下，展现出最为绚烂的色彩，与大暑热烈奔放的氛围相得益彰。
When copper is baptized by high temperature in the flame, it instantly blooms with dazzling green light. This green light is just like the vigorous vitality in the season of Great Heat. Under the excitation of high temperature, it shows the most gorgeous color, complementing the enthusiastic and unrestrained atmosphere of Great Heat.

实验目的
Experiment Purpose

证明铜在火焰中产生绿色，是物理变化。
To prove that the green color produced by copper in the flame is a physical change.

实验原理
Experiment Principle

在高温中，铜的电子以热能的形式吸收能量，跃迁更高等级的轨道。随后又以光能的形式放出能量，回到原来轨道。由于铜的电子轨道间距的特定，光的波长正好是绿色光的波长，由此产生了绿色的可见光。
At high temperatures, copper electrons absorb energy in the form of heat and jump to higher energy levels. They then release this energy as light and return to their original orbits. Due to the specific spacing of copper's electron orbits, the wavelength of the emitted light corresponds to green light, resulting in the observed green flame.

实验器材
Experiment Equipment

铜 Copper 酒精灯 Alcohol Lamp 蓝色钴玻璃 Blue Cobalt Glass 物理天平 Physical Balance 砂纸 Sandpaper

实验步骤
Experiment Procedures

  1. 用砂纸打磨掉铜表面的氧化层，并对铜进行称量
    Polish the copper surface with sandpaper to remove the oxide layer, and weigh the copper
  2. 用酒精灯对铜进行加热。
    Heat the copper with an alcohol lamp.
  3. 用蓝色鼓玻璃观察铜在被加热的过程中产生的绿色火焰
    Observe the green flame produced by the copper during heating through blue cobalt glass
  4. 查询资料对比同的发射光谱与观察到的光的颜色是否对应
    Compare the emission spectrum of copper with the observed color through reference materials
  5. 称量加热过的铜，比较加热前后的质量是否有变化
    Weigh the heated copper and compare its mass before and after heating

霜降与水的凝华实验
Frost's Descent and Sublimation of Water

霜降，预示着天气渐冷，初霜降临。此时进行水的凝华实验，仿佛是在复刻大自然的神奇变化。
Frost's Descent signals the gradual cooling of the weather and the arrival of the first frost. Conducting the sublimation experiment of water at this time seems to replicate the magical changes of nature.

在特定的环境下，水跳过液态，直接转变为固态的冰晶，这与霜降时节空气中的水汽在低温下直接凝结成霜的过程极为相似。那晶莹剔透的冰晶，就像清晨草木上凝结的白霜，见证着霜降节气带来的温度骤降，以及物质形态转变的奇妙瞬间。
Under specific conditions, water skips the liquid phase and directly transforms into solid ice crystals, which is very similar to the process by which water vapor in the air directly condenses into frost during Frost's Descent. The crystal-clear ice crystals, like the white frost on plants in the early morning, witness the sudden temperature drop brought by the Frost's Descent solar term and the奇妙 moment of material transformation.

实验目的
Experiment Purpose

观察水的凝华现象，了解气态水如何在低温环境下直接转变为固态冰。
To observe the sublimation phenomenon of water and understand how gaseous water directly transforms into solid ice under low-temperature conditions.

实验原理
Experiment Principle

气态水向低温处放出热量，凝华成为固态水。当气态水遇到温度足够低的物体表面时，会直接从气态转变为固态，形成冰晶。
Gaseous water releases heat to colder areas and sublimates into solid water. When gaseous water encounters a surface cold enough, it directly transforms from gas to solid, forming ice crystals.

实验器材
Experiment Equipment

固态二氧化碳（干冰） Solid Carbon Dioxide (Dry Ice) 试管 Test Tube 显微镜 Microscope 冰水混合物 Ice-Water Mixture

实验步骤
Experiment Procedures

  1. 取两只试管，分别放入适量冰水混合物和干冰，置于常温中观察
    Place appropriate amounts of ice-water mixture and dry ice in two test tubes respectively, and observe them at room temperature
  2. 出现霜和水珠/冰珠后，用显微镜观察结晶
    After frost and water droplets/ice beads appear, observe the crystals under a microscope
  3. 以确定试管上的冰是由水蒸气直接凝华得来的（由液态水凝固形成的冰与直接由水蒸气凝华形成的冰，晶体结构和形状会有所不同）
    To determine that the ice on the test tube is directly sublimated from water vapor (the crystal structure and shape of ice formed by solidification of liquid water are different from those formed by direct sublimation of water vapor)
